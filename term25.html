Inferior Good

When the economy is booming, people tend to have higher incomes and desire more lavish products. For example, people are more likely to buy Ferraris when they have money to spend. Since people are buying up the Ferraris, the demand for cars from Hyundai have gone down. Why would somebody want a Hyundai over a Ferrari when they have the money to afford the Ferrari? However, a recession soon hit. People no longer have the money to be picky about what car they want to purchase. Due to the decreased incomes of the general population, the demand for the Ferrari has decreased while the demand for the Hyundai cars have increased.
