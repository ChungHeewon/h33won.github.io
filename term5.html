Comparative Advantage

Pete Pelkey and Ben Recktenwald are both artists. Pete Pelkey can create 40 paintings or 20 drawings on any given day, while Ben Recktenwald can create 60 paintings or 60 drawings on any given day. If Pete wants to create 1 drawing, then he must give up on producing 2 paintings. Meanwhile, if Ben wants to create 1 drawing then he only has to give up on producing 1 drawing. Since Ben has to give up less than Pelkey to create a drawing, Ben has a comparative advantage in drawings. Basically, an individual or group has a comparative advantage in creating a specific product if they can create it at a lower opportunity cost than any other individual or group.
