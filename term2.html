Opportunity Cost

Pete Pelkey is a very busy man. He has many things to do and many places to be. Today, he wants to go to the library, go to the gym, and play video games. After thinking for a bit, Pete decides to go to the library. The opportunity cost of going to the library is going to the gym. By going to the library, Pete is giving up the next best option of going to the gym. Basically, the opportunity cost of doing one action is the next best option that you opted out of.
